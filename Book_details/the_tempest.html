The Tempest

"The Tempest" is a play written by William Shakespeare, believed to have been written in 1610-1611. It tells the story of Prospero, the rightful Duke of Milan, who was overthrown by his brother Antonio and exiled to a remote island with his daughter Miranda. Prospero has magical powers that he uses to manipulate the elements and the people who come to the island, including the shipwrecked sailors from Naples and his own treacherous brother.

Through the use of his magic, he orchestrates a series of events that lead to the eventual reconciliation of his family and the restoration of his position as Duke. The play deals with themes of betrayal, revenge, forgiveness, and the power of illusion and magic.
