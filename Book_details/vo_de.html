Vo De

"Vỡ đê" is a novel that reflects the social picture with full details and realistic lives of people under the period of slavery.

"Vỡ đê" consists of three parts with 25 chapters, presenting a multi-dimensional, truthful, and replicated perspective. The novel reflects the reality on a fairly wide range, from urban to rural areas, but focuses on criticizing the policies and domination tactics of the colonialists and officials that pushed the farmers into a situation of extreme poverty and hunger. This is a realistic and critical work of Vu Trong Phung, showing readers the dark days of the Vietnamese people under the domination of feudal colonialism during the period before 1945.

In "Vỡ đê," Vu Trong Phung praises the communists in the Lao động newspaper during the period of the grassroots front, the ones who boldly regarded prison as a training ground for creating warriors for humanity's struggle against those who had the role of exploiting the labor of others.
