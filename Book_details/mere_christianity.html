Mere Christianity

"Mere Christianity" is a book written by C.S. Lewis, a prominent Christian writer and theologian. The book was originally delivered as a series of radio broadcasts during World War II, and was later compiled and published as a book in 1952.

The book presents Lewis's arguments for the existence of God, and his views on the core teachings of Christianity. He discusses topics such as morality, the nature of God, free will, and the role of faith in the life of a believer. Lewis uses logic and reason to make his points, and the book is written in a clear and accessible style that makes it easy for readers to follow his arguments.

"Mere Christianity" has become a classic of Christian literature and has been widely read and discussed by believers and non-believers alike. Its appeal lies in its ability to articulate the core tenets of Christianity in a way that is both persuasive and accessible to a modern audience.
