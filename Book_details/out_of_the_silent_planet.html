Out of the silent planet

"Out of the Silent Planet" is the first novel in C.S. Lewis's "Space Trilogy," also known as the "Cosmic Trilogy" or "Ransom Trilogy." It was first published in 1938 and is a science fiction adventure story that follows the journey of a man named Dr. Elwin Ransom, who is kidnapped and taken to the planet Mars (called Malacandra by its inhabitants) by two human abductors. On Malacandra, Ransom encounters a variety of intelligent and unique creatures, including the Hrossa, the Sorns, and the Pfifltriggi, and he discovers a world vastly different from his own.

As Ransom navigates this strange new world, he learns about its culture, history, and spirituality, and he becomes embroiled in a conflict between the Malacandrans and a group of Earth's corrupt leaders who seek to exploit the planet's resources. Ransom ultimately becomes a key player in this conflict and must find a way to stop the Earthlings from destroying Malacandra.

The novel explores themes of colonialism, religion, and the nature of humanity, and it is considered a classic of science fiction literature
