The Death of Ivan Ilyich

"The Death of Ivan Ilyich" is a novella written by Russian author Leo Tolstoy, first published in 1886. The story focuses on the life and death of Ivan Ilyich, a high-ranking judge in 19th-century Russia, who leads a comfortable and unremarkable life until he is suddenly struck by a mysterious illness. As he grapples with his own mortality, Ivan Ilyich reflects on the choices he has made throughout his life, including his career, marriage, and relationships with family and friends.

Through Ivan Ilyich's journey, Tolstoy explores themes of the meaning of life, the pursuit of happiness, and the inevitability of death. The novella also examines the shallow and materialistic society of the time and the effects it has on individuals' lives. Overall, "The Death of Ivan Ilyich" is a powerful and thought-provoking work of literature that has become a classic in its own right.
