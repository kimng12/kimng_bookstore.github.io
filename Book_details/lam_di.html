Làm Đĩ

"Làm đĩ" is a famous novel-documentary by Vietnamese writer Vũ Trọng Phụng, published in 1937, a year after its completion. From its initial publications, the novel has sparked ongoing debates. Some view "Làm đĩ" as immoral and obscene, with a negative influence on readers' thoughts and morals. Others see it as a humanistic and poignant novel with high moral values.

The novel tells the life story of Huyền, a young girl who unknowingly falls into the path of becoming a "đĩ" (prostitute).

The book is divided into five parts: The Beginning, Adolescence, Birth, Marriage, and Debauchery, and The End. This basic structure provides guidance, conclusions, and prominent life philosophies in the three middle parts of the story.

Through the life story of a girl in the red-light district, Vũ Trọng Phụng depicts the reality of Vietnam during a transitional period, where traditional culture is fading in the face of Western influences. On one hand, there are trends of liberating oneself and women, following the free-spirited Western lifestyle. On the other hand, there are traditional women who are modest, proper, and conforming. Unable to uphold herself against the debauched lifestyle, Huyền falls into the path of becoming a prostitute. Therefore, it can be said that "Làm đĩ" is not only a humanistic and realistic story, but also a wake-up call for individuals to reflect on the cultural values of their country.
