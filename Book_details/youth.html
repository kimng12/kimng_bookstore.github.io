Youth

"Youth" is a short novel by Leo Tolstoy, first published in 1856. It tells the story of Dmitry Ivanovich, a young man from a wealthy family who embarks on a journey of self-discovery after experiencing disillusionment with his privileged lifestyle. The novel explores themes of youth, idealism, love, and the search for meaning in life.

As Dmitry travels through Russia, he encounters a variety of people and experiences that challenge his assumptions about the world. He falls in love with a young woman named Masha, but his idealistic visions of their future together are shattered when he realizes she does not share his beliefs.

Throughout the novel, Dmitry struggles to reconcile his desire for a meaningful life with the reality of his privileged upbringing. He comes to see that his wealth and status have shielded him from the struggles and hardships of ordinary people, and that true happiness can only be found through empathy and connection with others.

"Youth" is considered one of Tolstoy's early masterpieces, and is praised for its psychological depth and vivid portrayal of Russian society in the mid-19th century.
