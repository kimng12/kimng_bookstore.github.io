The Dead Zone

"The Dead Zone" is a novel written by Stephen King, first published in 1979. The story follows Johnny Smith, a young man who awakens from a five-year coma to discover he has acquired psychic powers. Johnny can see glimpses of the future and past, and when he touches people, he can see their secrets and memories.

As Johnny tries to come to terms with his new abilities, he becomes embroiled in a series of events that lead him to confront a dangerous politician who could trigger a catastrophic global conflict. The novel explores themes of destiny, free will, and the consequences of taking action or failing to do so.

King's signature blend of horror, suspense, and psychological drama is present throughout the novel, making it a compelling and thrilling read. The book was well-received upon its release and has since become a classic in the horror and thriller genres. It has been adapted into a successful film, as well as a television series.
